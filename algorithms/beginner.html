The beginner method solves the cube layer by layer using intuitive moves and a few simple algorithms. You only need to memorize 5-7 algorithms!

üìù Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1: White Cross

Goal: Create a white cross on the top face with edges matching the center colors.

No algorithm needed - intuitive solving

Tips: Solve each white edge piece one by one, making sure the side colors match the centers.

Step 2: White Corners (First Layer)

Goal: Complete the white face by solving the corner pieces.

R U R' U'

How to use: Position a white corner below its target spot and repeat this algorithm 2-4 times until it's solved.

Step 3: Second Layer Edges

Goal: Solve the four edge pieces in the middle layer.

To move edge to the RIGHT:
U R U' R' U' F' U F
To move edge to the LEFT:
U' L' U L U F U' F'

Recognition: Find an edge in the top layer without yellow, align it with the matching center, then use the appropriate algorithm.

Step 4: Yellow Cross

Goal: Create a yellow cross on the top face.

F R U R' U' F'

How to use: You might have a dot, L-shape, or line. Repeat this algorithm 1-3 times until you get a yellow cross.

Step 5: Position Yellow Edges

Goal: Make the yellow edges match the side centers.

R U R' U R U2 R'

How to use: Rotate U until at least 2 edges match their sides. Hold a matching edge in the back and do this algorithm. May need to repeat once.

Step 6: Position Yellow Corners

Goal: Get all yellow corners in the correct positions (colors may be wrong orientation).

U R U' L' U R' U' L

How to use: Find a corner that's in the right position (even if twisted). Hold it front-right and do this algorithm. Repeat if needed.

Step 7: Orient Yellow Corners (Final Step!)

Goal: Twist the yellow corners to solve the cube completely.

R' D' R D

How to use: Hold an unsolved corner in the front-right position. Repeat R' D' R D 2-4 times until that corner is solved. Then turn ONLY the U layer to bring the next unsolved corner to front-right and repeat. The cube will solve itself!

‚ùå Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Wrong color scheme: Make sure you know which colors are opposite (white-yellow, red-orange, green-blue)
  • Breaking solved parts: Be careful not to mess up already solved layers
  • Wrong algorithm direction: Remember R is clockwise, R' is counterclockwise
  • Giving up too soon: The last step (R' D' R D) looks messy but works if you keep going!
  • Not practicing enough: Muscle memory takes time - practice each step separately

üí° Success Tips for Beginners

  • Practice each step until you can do it without looking at instructions
  • Use a real cube - it's easier to learn than virtual simulators
  • Go slow and focus on accuracy before speed
  • If stuck, scramble and start over - it's great practice!
  • Celebrate small victories - completing each step is an achievement
